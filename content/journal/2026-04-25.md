I've started to have an individualistic outlook about technical work. At the beginning of my career, I overly emphasized the authority of my employer, as if it were the government or the education system. I didn't perceive a separation between my colleagues (as individuals) and the company. I didn't challenge senior engineers or founders because from my point of view, they *are* the company and their word is gospel.

Slowly, as I gained more expertise and started becoming in-charge of more decisions, I realized that this is not true: my creative output comes from *my* taste, personality and autonomy instead of being tied to an institution. And this led me to reframe my beliefs about positions of authority. I began seeing my workplace with a higher resolution, down to individuals: each employee is not a mere demographic; their work is far more consequential than they realize. Someone else may be making the decisions, but they are not infallible. [A crypto startup called 0xPPL](https://0xppl.com) I once worked at is shut down because of the unilateral way its founder ran it and the engineers slowly adjusted to not challenging the founder at all -- all of the key engineers left citing this.

If you've lost the feeling of agency at work, I urge you to introspect: who has the power to take decisions for you and why?
